RAM Price Crisis Driven by AI

05‑03‑2026

Explosive memory demand caused by artificial intelligence

Since the rise of large generative AI models, RAM demand has seen exponential growth. This unprecedented increase directly impacts consumer pricing.

📈 AI impact on RAM demand

  • Generative AI models: language models like GPT, Gemini, and Claude require from 16 GB to several TB of RAM
  • Model training: researchers and companies invest heavily in high-capacity servers (256 GB to 1 TB+)
  • Local inference: users increasingly run AI models locally, raising RAM requirements
  • Massive datasets: AI data processing needs unprecedented memory capacity

💰 Observed price increases

  • DDR5 16 GB: +45% in 2 years (2024–2026)
  • DDR5 32 GB: +52% in 2 years
  • DDR5 64 GB: +58% in 2 years
  • Server ECC RAM: +60% in 2 years for high-capacity modules

🏭 Production bottlenecks

  • Limited manufacturing capacity: NAND and DRAM fabs are running near full load
  • Rare raw materials: competition for silicon wafers is intensifying
  • Global supply chain pressure: geopolitical tensions complicate distribution
  • Insufficient short-term investment: production cannot scale fast enough

🎯 Main affected players

Major manufacturers:

  • Samsung Electronics
  • SK Hynix
  • Micron Technology
  • Intel Optane

⚡ Crucial’s withdrawal from the consumer RAM market

One major consequence of this crisis is the gradual withdrawal of Crucial from consumer RAM production. Crucial, a long-standing Micron brand, announced a phased stop of mainstream RAM lines in 2025–2026.

  • Why: profit margins became too weak versus dominant Asian competition (Samsung, SK Hynix)
  • Strategic shift: Micron focuses on high-end and server segments, leaving mainstream consumer PCs
  • Market impact: lower supply and stronger concentration around three major Asian players
  • Consumer impact: less choice, less competition, and stronger upward pressure on prices

Affected sectors:

  • Data centers and cloud computing
  • Academic research and AI companies
  • Gamers and content creators
  • PC/server/laptop manufacturers
  • Consumer electronics

📊 Future outlook (2026–2027)

  • Expected stabilization: new TSMC and Samsung fabs should add around 30% capacity in 2026
  • More efficient generations: DDR6 and GDDR7 could reduce raw memory needs
  • Model optimization: smaller AI models may reduce large-memory demand
  • Increased competition: new entrants may put downward pressure on prices

💡 Buyer tips

  • Wait if possible: prices may drop 15–25% by 2027
  • Choose trusted RAM brands: Corsair, Kingston, G.Skill often provide stronger warranty support
  • Consider used market: second-hand can save 20–30%
  • Prefer DDR5 over DDR4: DDR5 is the long-term path despite a higher current cost
  • Buy modularly: purchase what you need now rather than over-specifying immediately

🔮 Conclusion

The AI revolution created a perfect storm for RAM pricing: massive demand, limited production, and low short-term flexibility. While this crisis is real and affects IT budgets, conditions should improve from 2027 onward. Current manufacturing investments and AI model efficiency gains are expected to ease pressure. In the meantime, careful planning and market awareness are essential.
